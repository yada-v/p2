We now translate the geometry into equations.

Step 1 — Velocity gradient

\[ \frac{du}{dy} = \frac{U}{h} \]

Step 2 — Newton's law of viscosity

Fluid resistance (shear stress) is proportional to the gradient:

\[ \tau = \eta \frac{du}{dy} , \tau = \eta \frac{U}{h} \]

Result — Required Force

\[ F = \tau A = \eta A \frac{U}{h} \]

Force grows with viscosity, plate area, and speed;decreases as gap widens.
