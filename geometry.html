Geometric Insight

Velocity is not the same everywhere:

  • At the bottom: velocity = \(0\)
  • At the top: velocity = \(v\)
  • Between them: velocity increases smoothly

So across distance \(h\) , velocity changes from \(0\) to \(h\). This creates a velocity gradient: $$\frac{dv}{dy} = \frac{v}{h}$$
