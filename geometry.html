Geometric Insight

Velocity is not the same everywhere in the gap:

  • At the bottom plate: \(u = 0\)
  • At the top plate: \(u = U\)
  • In between: velocity increases linearly

So across gap height \(h\), velocity changes from \(0\) to \(U\). This produces a uniform velocity gradient:

\[ \frac{du}{dy} = \frac{U}{h} \]

This gradient is the key quantity — it tells us how rapidly layers of fluid slide past one another, which is directly linked to the internal friction force.
