The drag force is given by $$F_d = \frac{1}{2} C_d A \rho v^2$$, where \(C_d\) is the drag coefficient, \(A\) is cross-sectional area, \(\rho\) is density of the medium, and \(v\) is velocity. The equation of motion is $$m \frac{dv}{dt} = mg - F_d$$. At terminal velocity, \(dv/dt = 0\), so \(mg = F_d\). Substituting the drag force and solving for \(v_t\) yields the terminal velocity: $$v_t = \sqrt{\frac{2mg}{C_d A \rho}}$$
